1. The writ of prohibition can only be used to.prevent the doing of some act
which is about to be done, and can never be used as a remedy for acts
already completed.
2. Therefore, where the court to which the writ should be issued, has already
disposed of the case, so that nothing remains which that court can do,
either by way of executing its judgment or otherwise, no prohibition will
be granted.
3. And this is true, though the final disposition of the case was made after
service on the judge of a rule to show cause why the writ should not
issue, and though other cases of the same character may be pending in
the same court.

for a writ of prohibition to the judge of the District Court
of the Northern District of California, to prevent that court
