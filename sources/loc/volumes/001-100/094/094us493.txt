The final judgment in this case was rendered Oct. 8, 1876,
and the writ of error issued Nov. 16, returnable to the
October merm, 18t7. The defendants in error, having filed
a copy of the record and docketed the cause, now move to dismiss, for want of jurisdiction.
It is not claimed by the plaintiff in error that there is any
Federal question disclosed by the record, but it ii insisted that
a motion to dismiss cannot be entertained until the return-day
of the writ. Such was the old practice; but in Exparte -Russel,
13 Wall. 671, and Thomas v. Wooldridge, 23 id. 288, the rule
was changed. It seemed to us then that such a change would
"be likely to prevent great delays and expense, and further
