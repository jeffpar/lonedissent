DECISIONS PER CURIAM.
391 U. S.

June 3, 1968.

VAUGHN v. RODRIGUEZ, ACTING WARDEN.
ON PETITION FOR WRIT OF CERTIORARI TO THE UNITED
STATES COURT OF APPEALS FOR THE TENTH CIRCUIT.

No. 1149, Misc.

Decided June 3, 1968.

Certiorari granted; vacated and remanded.

Boston E. Witt, Attorney General of New Mexico, and
Thomas 0. Olson, Special Assistant Attorney General,
for respondent.
PER CURIAM.

The motion for leave to proceed in forma pauperis and
the petition for a writ of certiorari are granted. The
judgment is vacated and the case is remanded to the
United States Court of Appeals for the Tenth Circuit
for further consideration in light of Peyton v. Rowe,
ante, p. 54.
PEART v. CALIFORNIA.
APPEAL FROM THE SUPREME COURT OF CALIFORNIA.

No. 1445, Misc.

Decided June 3, 1968.

67 Cal. 2d 866, 434 P. 2d 609, appeal dismissed and certiorari
denied.

PER CURIAM.

The appeal is dismissed for want of jurisdiction.
Treating the papers whereon the appeal was taken as a
petition for a writ of certiorari, certiorari is denied.
